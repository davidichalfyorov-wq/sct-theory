# Predictions: Background Briefing

Spectral Causal Theory produces testable predictions through three
mechanisms: (1) the modified graviton propagator, which alters
gravitational dynamics at distances comparable to 1/Lambda; (2) the
spectral action, which fixes the gravitational effective action and
thereby determines black hole thermodynamics and cosmological
perturbation spectra; and (3) the spectral dimension flow, which
provides a distinct UV signature measurable against lattice
quantum gravity programs.

## The modified graviton propagator

The dressed spin-2 propagator is

  G_TT(k^2) = 1 / [k^2 * Pi_TT(z)]

with Pi_TT(z) = 1 + (13/60) z F_hat_1(z), where z = k^2/Lambda^2.
This entire-function dressing introduces Yukawa corrections to the
Newtonian potential:

  V(r)/V_N(r) = 1 - (4/3) e^{-m_2 r} + (1/3) e^{-m_0 r}

with m_2 = Lambda sqrt(60/13) and m_0(xi) depending on the Higgs
non-minimal coupling. At conformal coupling xi = 1/6, the scalar
mode decouples (m_0 -> infinity). These corrections are exponentially
suppressed at r >> 1/Lambda and produce no observable effect in
current solar system tests (PPN gamma = 1 to all accessible precision).

## Prediction channels

Seven distinct prediction channels exist, each with its own
methodology and target dataset:

1. **QNM shifts (LT-3a).** The nonlocal SCT propagator modifies
   the effective potential in the Regge-Wheeler and Zerilli equations.
   This shifts quasinormal mode frequencies of black hole ringdown
   by amounts proportional to (Lambda * r_s)^{-2}. Target data:
   LVK O4/O5 ringdown catalogs, LISA forecasts.

2. **GW propagation (LT-3b).** The gravitational wave speed c_T = c
   is already proven (NT-4c). Frequency-dependent dispersion from
   the nonlocal form factor has not been computed. Secondary GW
   spectrum from inflationary perturbations requires INF-1 input.
   Target data: NANOGrav 15yr, EPTA DR2, LISA.

3. **Cosmological w(z) (LT-3c).** The modified Friedmann equations
   from NT-4c produce corrections to the dark energy equation of state.
   MT-2 showed these corrections are of order 10^{-64} at accessible
   Lambda, making them undetectable. Quantitative MCMC confirmation
   against Planck+DESI+Pantheon+ would close this channel formally.
   Target data: DESI DR1, Planck 2018, Pantheon+, KiDS-1000.

4. **Laboratory/Solar System (LT-3d).** Yukawa corrections constrain
   Lambda >= 2.565 meV (Eoet-Wash). Casimir effect gives
   Lambda >= 2.38 x 10^{-3} eV. These are parameter-free bounds
   (alpha = -4/3 is exact). COMPLETE: 85 tests, 5 figures.

5. **Stellar structure (LT-3e).** Modified TOV equations from the
   nonlocal field equations. The maximum neutron star mass must
   exceed 2.14 M_sun (PSR J0740+6620). Failure = falsification.

6. **Running constants (META-1).** The spectral action fixes initial
   conditions for c_1, c_2 at mu = Lambda. Running under RG flow
   produces specific trajectories that can be compared with asymptotic
   safety fixed-point values and CDT lattice measurements.

7. **Cross-program comparison (COMP-1).** Unified quantitative
   comparison of SCT with LQG, AS, CDT, string theory, and IDG
   across spectral dimension, singularity resolution, dispersion,
   PPN parameters, UV behavior, and matter coupling.
