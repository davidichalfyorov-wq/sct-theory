# OP-18: Dilaton inflation excluded

## 1. Statement

The trace mode of the Dirac operator in the product spectral triple
M x F yields a scalar degree of freedom (the spectral dilaton) coupled
to all SM fields through the spectral action. Determine whether the
dilaton can serve as the inflaton. The answer is negative: five
independent structural obstacles each individually prevent dilaton
inflation within the SM-only spectral action.

## 2. Context

If the scalaron mass problem (OP-17) cannot be resolved, the dilaton
is the only other scalar degree of freedom intrinsic to the spectral
triple. Unlike external inflatons added by hand, the dilaton arises
from the geometry of the finite spectral triple (A_F, H_F, D_F) and
inherits its couplings from the spectral action. A successful dilaton
inflation model would be a prediction, not an input.

The investigation (INF-2) found five independent obstacles, each of
which would individually prevent slow-roll inflation. The obstacles
are structural: they follow from the form of the spectral action and
the SM particle content, not from parameter choices.

## 3. Known Results

The five structural obstacles are:

1. **Flat tree-level potential.** The spectral action Tr(f(D^2/Lambda^2))
   produces a kinetic term for the dilaton but no potential at tree level.
   The dilaton appears only through the overall conformal factor of the
   metric, and the spectral action is conformally invariant at leading
   order in d = 4.

2. **Phi-independent masses.** SM particle masses in the spectral triple
   are determined by the Yukawa couplings and the Higgs VEV, not by the
   dilaton VEV. This means the Coleman-Weinberg one-loop potential has
   no dependence on the dilaton field phi.

3. **No anomaly-driven potential.** In some theories (e.g., technicolor),
   the trace anomaly generates an effective potential for a pseudo-dilaton.
   In the spectral action, the trace anomaly is the standard SM anomaly
   (proportional to beta functions of gauge couplings), which does not
   couple to the spectral dilaton.

4. **Coleman-Weinberg shape excluded.** Even if a one-loop potential were
   generated by some mechanism, its Coleman-Weinberg shape (V ~ phi^4
   log(phi)) is ruled out by BICEP/Keck 2021 data at more than 95% CL.
   The predicted tensor-to-scalar ratio r > 0.1 exceeds the bound
   r < 0.036.

5. **R^2 produces scalaron, not dilaton.** The R^2 term in the spectral
   action gives rise to a massive spin-0 mode (scalaron) that is distinct
   from the dilaton. The scalaron is a curvature degree of freedom; the
   dilaton is a matter degree of freedom. They do not mix at linear order.

## 4. Failed Approaches

1. **Conformal symmetry breaking.** Attempted to generate a dilaton
   potential through explicit conformal symmetry breaking via the spectral
   cutoff Lambda. The breaking is present (Lambda introduces a mass scale)
   but affects only the scalaron sector (R^2 term), not the dilaton
   kinetic sector.

2. **Finite spectral triple fluctuations.** Investigated whether inner
   fluctuations of the finite Dirac operator D_F could generate a
   dilaton-dependent potential. The inner fluctuations produce Higgs-type
   potentials, not dilaton potentials: the Higgs field is a component of
   the connection, while the dilaton (if present) is a metric degree of
   freedom.

3. **Non-minimal coupling enhancement.** Tried to use a large xi coupling
   to amplify dilaton effects. This modifies the scalaron mass (OP-17
   territory) but does not generate a dilaton potential, because the xi
   term couples R to the Higgs field H, not to the dilaton.

## 5. Success Criteria

- Identify a BSM extension of the spectral triple that generates a
  viable dilaton potential compatible with CMB constraints.
- OR: provide a rigorous no-go theorem for dilaton inflation within
  any spectral triple satisfying the Chamseddine-Connes axioms.
- The extension must specify the finite algebra A_F', Hilbert space
  H_F', and Dirac operator D_F', and compute the resulting inflationary
  observables (n_s, r, f_NL).

## 6. Suggested Directions

1. **Extended spectral triples.** The Pati-Salam spectral triple
   A_F = C + H + M_2(H) + M_4(C) has additional scalar fields. Check
   whether any of these can serve as a dilaton with a non-trivial
   potential.

2. **Grand Unification spectral triples.** SO(10) or E_6 spectral
   triples contain additional scalar multiplets. Compute the spectral
   action for these and extract the scalar potential.

3. **External EFT input.** Accept that the spectral action alone does
   not produce inflation and parameterize the inflationary sector as
   an effective field theory input. This is honest but reduces the
   predictivity of the framework.

4. **Non-perturbative effects.** If Postulate 5 is formulated as a
   path integral over spectral triples (V3), non-perturbative effects
   could generate a dilaton potential through instantons or tunnelling.
   This requires OP-02 to be resolved first.

## 8. Connections

- **Blocked by OP-17:** if the scalaron mass problem is solved, dilaton
  inflation becomes unnecessary (Starobinsky inflation with correct mass
  would be the viable channel).
- **OP-02 (Postulate 5):** non-perturbative dilaton potential requires
  a specified dynamical principle.
- **OP-19 (late-time cosmology):** independent problem, but both
  illustrate the difficulty of extracting cosmological predictions from
  a UV-complete framework.
- The five obstacles are independent of Gap G1 (OP-01): the dilaton
  sector involves only conformal and Higgs degrees of freedom, not the
  Weyl tensor.
