# Literature Map

Papers organised by topic with specific equation/theorem references.

## Spectral Action and NCG Foundations

- **Chamseddine, Connes (1996)** hep-th/9606001
  The spectral action principle. Eq. (1.1): S = Tr(f(D^2/Lambda^2)).
  Theorem 1.1: heat-kernel expansion recovers Einstein-Hilbert + SM.

- **Chamseddine, Connes, Marcolli (2006)** hep-th/0610241
  Gravity and the SM with neutrino mixing. Section 4: spectral triple
  for full SM. Eq. (4.3): Higgs sector from finite geometry.

- **van Suijlekom (2015)** ISBN 978-94-017-9162-5
  Textbook: Noncommutative Geometry and Particle Physics.
  Chapter 11: spectral action computation.

## Heat Kernel and Form Factors

- **Barvinsky, Vilkovisky (1985)** Phys. Rep. 119, 1
  Covariant perturbation theory. Eq. (3.45): general form factor structure.
  Section 6: alpha-insertion technique for functional derivatives.

- **Codello, Zanusso (2010)** arXiv:1002.2188
  One-loop form factors in the EH action. Table 1: h_C and h_R for
  scalars, fermions, vectors. Eq. (3.8): scalar h_C.

- **Calmet, Prester, Radovanovic (2008)** arXiv:0805.2909
  SM counting: N_s=4, N_D=22.5, N_v=12. Table II: multiplicities.
  Convention: N_f = 2N_D = 45 for 4-component Dirac fermions.

## Causal Set Theory

- **Bombelli, Lee, Meyer, Sorkin (1987)** Phys. Rev. Lett. 59, 521
  Founding paper. Definition of causal set. Hauptvermutung.

- **Surya (2019)** arXiv:1903.11544
  Review article. Section 3: kinematics. Section 5: dynamics.

## Benincasa-Dowker Action

- **Benincasa, Dowker (2010)** arXiv:1001.2725
  BD action on causal sets. Eq. (16): c_d coefficients.
  Table 1: c_2 = 2, c_3 = 6/sqrt(6), c_4 = 4/sqrt(6).

- **Belenchia, Benincasa, Dowker (2016)** arXiv:1510.04656
  Continuum limit proof. Theorem 3.1: BD action -> Box - R/2.

- **de Brito, Eichhorn, Pfeiffer (2023)** arXiv:2301.13525
  Higher-order causal set actions. Theorem 4.2: BD^2 -> R^2 - 2BoxR.
  Eq. (59)-(60): Poisson factorisation of stacked layer sums.
  Section 5: coefficient matrix C_i C_j for the stacked sector.

## Diamond Geometry and Curvature

- **Wang (2019)** arXiv:1904.01034
  Causal diamond volumes and Weyl tensor. Proposition 5.2:
  ACD area deficit proportional to E^2 in d=4. Eq. (5.8): H^2 = 6(d-4).
  Key result: magnetic Weyl drops out exclusively in d=4.

- **Gibbons, Solodukhin (2007)** hep-th/0703098
  Small causal diamond geometry. Eq. (2.8): volume expansion
  V = V_flat(1 - R/42 tau^2 + ...). Eq. (3.1): area expansion.

- **Martinetti, Rovelli (2003)** gr-qc/0212074
  Diamond temperature. Eq. (17): T_d = 1/(pi T).
  Section 3: Unruh effect for bounded trajectories.

## Chain Counting and Intervals

- **Roy, Sinha, Surya (2013)** arXiv:1212.0631
  Discrete geometry of small causal diamond. Section 3: chain
  abundances via nested integrals. Eq. (3.5): C_k expectation.
  Section 4: Ricci scalar extraction from 2-chains.

- **Johnston (2008)** arXiv:0806.3083
  Particle propagators on discrete spacetime. Eq. (7): resolvent
  K = I + Phi(I - bPhi)^{-1}. Section 4: path-sum interpretation.

- **Johnston (2009)** arXiv:0909.0944
  Feynman propagator on causal set. Link matrix definition.

## Ghosts and Unitarity

- **Anselmi (2017)** arXiv:1704.07728
  Fakeon prescription. Theorem 2: fakeon propagator gives zero
  production amplitude at ghost poles. Section 4: polynomial case proof.

- **Anselmi, Piva (2018)** arXiv:1803.07777
  Quantum gravity with fakeons. Extension to Lee-Wick models.

- **Kubo, Kugo (2023)** arXiv:2308.09006
  Kubo-Kugo objection. Eq. (3.12): KO quartet mechanism. Section 4:
  spin-2 vs spin-1 mismatch argument.

## Superenergy and Bel-Robinson

- **Senovilla (2000)** gr-qc/9906087
  Super-energy tensors review. Eq. (3.1): Bel-Robinson tensor
  T_{abcd} = C_{aecf}C_b^e{}_d^f + *C_{aecf} *C_b^e{}_d^f.
  Proposition 3.3: T_{abcd} u^a u^b u^c u^d >= 0 (dominant property).

## Black Hole Entropy

- **Solodukhin (2008)** arXiv:0802.3117
  Entanglement entropy and conformal invariance. Eq. (1.2): universal
  logarithmic term c_log = (1/180)(N_s + 6N_f + 12N_v).

## Spectral Dimension

- **Lauscher, Reuter (2005)** hep-th/0508202
  Spectral dimension in asymptotic safety. Result: d_S -> 2 in UV.
  Eq. (4.3): return probability definition.

## Swampland

- **Ooguri, Vafa (2018)** arXiv:1810.05506
  Refined de Sitter conjecture. Eq. (1): |nabla V| >= c V or
  min(nabla_i nabla_j V) <= -c' V.
