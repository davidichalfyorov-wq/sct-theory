# Foundations: Background Briefing

Spectral Causal Theory (SCT) is a candidate framework for quantum gravity
based on the spectral action principle of Chamseddine and Connes. The
classical action is

  S = Tr(f(D²/Λ²)) + ⟨ψ, Dψ⟩

where D is the Dirac operator of a spectral triple (A, H, D), f is a
positive even cutoff function, and Λ is the spectral cutoff scale.

## The five postulates

1. Spacetime is described by a spectral triple (A, H, D).
2. The Standard Model is encoded in the finite spectral triple
   (A_F, H_F, D_F) with A_F = C ⊕ H ⊕ M₃(C).
3. The gravitational sector is the product geometry M × F.
4. Physical observables are spectral invariants of D.
5. Dynamics is determined by extremization of the spectral action
   (variant V1), spectral flow (V2), or path integral over
   spectral triples (V3). **This postulate is not yet specified.**

## What has been derived

The one-loop effective action is fully computed:

  Γ = (1/2)∫ d⁴x √g [α_C C² + α_R R²]

with α_C = 13/120 (parameter-free, from SM content N_s=4, N_f=45, N_v=12)
and α_R(ξ) = 2(ξ−1/6)² (depends on Higgs non-minimal coupling ξ).

The nonlinear field equations, FLRW reduction, linearised propagator,
and modified Newtonian potential have been derived. The theory is
perturbatively UV-finite at one and two loops (D=0).

## Key open structural issues

- Postulate 5 is unspecified (three variants, none investigated).
- UV-completeness fails at three loops (3:1 overdetermination).
- Gap G1: the Weyl-sector variational correction Θ^(C) is not
  computed on non-trivially curved backgrounds.
- The cutoff function f is not fixed by any postulate.
