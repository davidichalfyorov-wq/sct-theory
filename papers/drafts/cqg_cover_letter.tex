\begin{letter}{Editor\\Classical and Quantum Gravity}

\opening{Dear Editor,}

We submit the manuscript ``Nonlocal one-loop form factors from spectral causal theory'' for consideration in Classical and Quantum Gravity.

The paper computes the complete nonlocal one-loop form factors $F_1$ and $F_2$ of the curvature-squared effective action for the full Standard Model particle content within the spectral action framework. Using Barvinsky--Vilkovisky covariant perturbation theory and the Codello--Zanusso diagrammatic heat kernel, we derive closed-form results for each spin sector in the Weyl basis $\{C^2, R^2\}$.

The main results are: the local Weyl-squared coefficient $\alpha_C = 13/120$ and $R$-squared coefficient $\alpha_R = 2(\xi - 1/6)^2$; entireness of both form factors, which excludes additional propagator poles at one loop; the scalar graviton decoupling condition at conformal coupling; and a modified Newtonian potential with calculable effective masses. All analytic results are checked by independent numerical evaluation.

The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere.

\closing{Sincerely,}
